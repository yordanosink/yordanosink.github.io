Drift

The sky forgets its former blue,
dissolving into something new.

I walk between what fades and stays,
a quiet ghost of borrowed days.

Stillness

Nothing moves and yet it breathes,
hidden under subtle leaves.

Time softens at the edges here,
becoming less and less severe.

On Attention

Attention is the rarest form of generosity. In a world saturated with noise, to truly notice something is almost radical.

We scroll, we skim, we glance—but we rarely stay. And yet, meaning only reveals itself to those who linger.

Perhaps the problem is not that life lacks depth, but that we refuse to go deep.

The Shape of Memory

Memory does not archive—it edits. It softens certain edges and sharpens others. What remains is not truth, but something more personal.

Night Conversation

A: Do you ever feel like time is watching you?

B: I think it’s the other way around.

A: And what do we do with it?

B: We pretend it’s not slipping.

The Door

She: You never opened it.

He: I was waiting.

She: For what?

He: For it to open itself.
