Drift

The sky forgets its former blue,
dissolving into something new.

I walk between what fades and stays,
a quiet ghost of borrowed days.

Stillness

Nothing moves and yet it breathes,
hidden under subtle leaves.

Time softens at the edges here,
becoming less and less severe.

On Attention

Attention is the rarest form of generosity. In a world saturated with noise, to truly notice something is almost radical.

We scroll, we skim, we glance—but we rarely stay.

“The most valuable thing you can give someone is your undivided attention.”
